Movie Poster — Zaara

Visual storytelling & poster design for the short film “Zaara”. The poster explores mood, character and minimalist type composition to reflect the film’s tone.

Role: Poster composition, color grading, typography.
Tools: Photoshop, Illustrator.

Concept

For this project, I designed two posters for the short film ‘Zaara’,
directed by Jeet Singhvi. exploring how visual storytelling can capture
the film’s emotional arc. My goal was to translate the couple’s journey—
struggle, separation, and rediscovery—into compelling imagery. Each poster
highlights a different mood and moment, using composition, color, and
symbolism to reflect the themes of vulnerability, resilience, and hope at
the heart of the story.

About the Film

Zaara follows a couple struggling after the husband’s paralytic stroke.
She pushes herself to help him walk again but loses hope and leaves. They
reunite in a temple, where he learns she is pregnant—bringing them back
together and renewing their sense of love, responsibility, and healing.

Personal Takeaways
Designing the Zaara posters taught me how deeply
visuals can hold emotion. Translating the couple’s struggle and reconciliation into imagery
pushed me to think sensitively about mood, symbolism, and restraint. This project strengthened
my ability to communicate complex narratives through subtle, meaningful visual choices.
The client requesed, simple imagery with text. So the process for me was mainly figuring out
how to make the best use of the stills and images of the movie to convey the best message possible.
I also had fun experimenting with different fonts and the lettering for the title.
