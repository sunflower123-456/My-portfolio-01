NGO Re-Branding — Sightsavers India

Role: Visual identity, logo, emblem and pattern design.

Project Overview

This project involved designing a new logo for a non-governmental organization of my choice. I selected Sightsavers India Foundation, a non-profit working to prevent avoidable blindness, restore sight, and promote equal opportunities for people with visual impairments.

Process

Sightsavers’ current logo features two interlinked golden loops forming a continuous shape, symbolizing connection, support, and infinity. The yellow color represents light, optimism, and hope. The design functions well because: It is memorable and minimal, easy to reproduce across formats. The looping form subtly communicates unity and inclusivity. The color evokes warmth and positivity, aligning with the organization’s message. However, it does not fully communicate who the organization serves. Without prior knowledge, the logo doesn’t directly hint at vision, sight, or accessibility. It feels corporate and abstract, leaving room for more emotional and human-centered storytelling.

Brainstorming

Explored visual metaphors and symbolic elements related to the name, and values like, sight, care, inclusion.

Finalising the logo

The new logo for Sight savers India combines empathy and clarity through the hands cradling an eye, forming an ‘S’—symbolizing care, protection, and human connection. The Braille inspired dot in the eye depicts inclusivity. The Cocogoose Pro Light typeface adds warmth and clarity, while the color palette blends optimism, trust, and depth—visually reflecting Sightsavers India’s mission of empathy, accessibility, and equal opportunity for all.

What I learned & next steps: short reflection on accessibility considerations and design decisions.

Personal Takeaways

Through this project, I learned how design can communicate care and inclusion beyond visuals. Translating empathy into form challenged me to think sensitively about accessibility and symbolism. It taught me that meaningful design isn’t just seen, it’s felt, connecting people through understanding, awareness, and purpose-driven creativity.
